    Center for Media and Public Affairs (CMPA)
    Studies news and entertainment media using what it calls "scientific content analysis. CMPA also conducts surveys and focus groups to illuminate the media's role in structuring the public agenda." The problem with this is that there is nothing "scientific" about "content analysis." CMPA is a deeply hypocritical organization which pretends to be "objective, empirical and impartial" when it fact it is a right-wing organization with a permanent agenda of hostility to environmentalists and consumer activists. On the other hand, its reports (it likes to call them "monographs") do contain a lot of empirical information, and I take it seriously for this reason.

    This site is an online host to "magazine media kits" -- the promotional information which magazines use to attract advertisers.     La Prensa
    During the 1970s, this newspaper and its editor, Pedro Joaquin Chamorro, were leading opponents of the Somoza dictatorship. Following Chamorro's assassination and the Sandinista revolution that overthrew Somoza, the Chamorro family split, with the pro-Sandinista faction leaving to form a rival paper, El Nuevo Diario. Others, including Pedro Joaquin's widow, Violeta, became leading Sandinista opponents, and their political influence is what has shaped the La Prensa of today.

    Wisconsin Coordinating Council on Nicaragua (WCCN)
    WCCN is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization which coordinates people-to-people exchange programs and humanitarian assistance as part of Wisconsin's historic "sister state" relationship with Nicaragua. WCCN's programs include the Nicaraguan Credit Alternative Fund (NICA Fund), which channels loans from U.S. investors to provide credit for economic development in low-income Nicaraguan communities; and the Women's Empowerment Project, which organizes educational exchanges linking U.S. and Nicaraguan groups working on issues that affect women in both countries, such as poverty and domestic violence.

Nuclear

    Amarillo National Resource Center for Plutonium
    Sponsored by the U.S. Department of Energy, the Amarillo National Resource Center for Plutonium advocates using the plutonium from dismantled nuclear weapons "in such a way that it can never be used again in weapons." Its idea? "Mixing it with uranium and using as fuel for nuclear power plants." This site also provides access to scientific studies and other information about plutonium.
