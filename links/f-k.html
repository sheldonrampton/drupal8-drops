Food/Farming

    Center for Biologics Evaluation and Research
    The Center for Biologics Evaluation and Research (CBER) is an agency of the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) responsible for assessing safety and effectiveness of pharmaceuticals. Their website has meeting transcripts, announcement of product approvals and withdrawals, and a variety of other related documents.
    Center for Science in the Public Interest
    The CSPI is the group that called fettucine alfredo "a heart attack on a plate." Lately they've been campaigning against Olestra (aka Olean), the "nonfattening fat" developed by Proctor & Gamble. The National Restaurant Association is gunning for these folks, along with CSPI's arch-rival, the corporate-funded American Council on Science and Health. The CSPI website has lots of good information including their newsletter and recipes for healthy eating.
    Epicurious
    More than 8,000 recipes from Bon Appétit and Gourmet magazine, plus hundreds of drink recipes, a food dictionary and information on wines.
    FDA Center for Food Safety & Applied Nutrition
    Information on food safety issues from the point of view of the U.S. Food and Drug Administration.
    National Milk Producers Federation
    The world according to the dairy industry.
    FoxBGHsuit
    Investigative journalists Steve Wilson and Jane Akre were fired for reporting on the Monsanto corporation's genetically-engineered recombinant bovine growth hormone (rBGH). This website tells the story.
    The McLibel Trial
    In 1990, the McDonalds hamburger chain filed a libel lawsuit against two environmental activists who had helped circulate a leaflet criticizing the company on environmental and health grounds. The lawsuit dragged on for seven years, sparking an international campaign in support of free speech and exposing numerous sordid facts about the company's practices, including its use of paid infiltrators to spy on its critics. This website documents what happened during the lawsuit in exquisite detail.
    VegSource -- Your Friendly Vegetarian Resource!
    In addition to political discussion and health information, this site features 5,000 online recipes.

Government & Politics

    American Association of Retired Persons
    The leading association of senior citizens in the U.S. This site includes their positions on political issues of concern such as health care and social security, along with volunteer opportunities. AARP also provides an internet grassroots advocacy page that lets people send email to their federal elected officials.
    CapWeb
    Internet guide to the U.S. Congress. Numerous resources, including a page of links to various political watchdog groups.
    The Coin-Operated Congress
    Investigative reports on congressional campaign financing, from Mother Jones magazine.
    Colombia Support Network
    Human rights information about the country of Colombia.
    Congress.Org -- Your Link to Congress
    This website was created by Issue Dynamics, Inc., a grassroots PR firm. It includes a locater for email addresses of Washington politicians that lets you send them email.
    Congressional Research Service
    This provides access to reports written by the CRS.
    The Conservative Activist's Home Page
    Federal Election Contributions
    This website was put together by the guy who used to run the website for the Federal Elections Commission. He got tired of the bureaucracy and decided to create his own independent site, which is easier to use. Like the FEC page, it lets you look up the amounts and sources of campaign financing for individual candidates, and also has information about "soft money." Additional information about campaign financing is available at a website run by American University.
    FEDIX: Key Federal Agencies
    Federal Information Exchange (FEDIX) is a free on-line information retrieval service of federal funding opportunities for the education and research communities. Key federal agencies such as the National Institutes for Health, Department of Defense, and US Agency for International Development support FEDIX under a cooperative agreement from the Department of Energy (which, ironically, was not cooperating itself with FEDIX when I visited last on 5/2/98).
    Government Accounting Office
    The GAO publishes reports, often containing information unavailable through other sources, for congress and other public agencies. Topics covered are diverse.
    Government Printing Office Access / Government Documents
    Searchable access to the Federal Register, Congressional Record and many other important government documents. This site is a real treasure trove for doing research.
    Historical Atlas of the 20th Century
    A fascinating collection of information about living conditions, forms of government and war casualties during the 20th century.
    Magellan Politics Web Guide
    An extensive list of brief reviews of websites dealing with political topics.
    NetAction
    Ideas and resources to help internet activists have greater impact.
    Project Vote Smart voting records
    Facts about elected officials and issues, including biographies, campaign finance information, issue analysis and more.
    Protest.Net
    Posts information about hundreds of protests, meetings and conferences.
    THOMAS -- U.S. Congress on the Internet
    Text of bills before Congress, the Congressional Record, and a section titled "how laws are passed." A good source for basic information, but of course there's no analysis here. Government websites get in trouble with someone if they say anything controversial. For that matter, I have yet to seen a politics website that makes even a half-hearted effort to keep track of the activities of lobbyists.
    ZNet
    A leftist website maintained by Z Magazine.

Graphics/Desktop Publishing

    American Memory from the Library of Congress
    Downloadable photographs, movies and audio recordings of American historical themes.
    DesktopPublishing.com
    Offers online clip art, organized by subject and searchable.
    Feature Photo Service
    Puts publicity photos on the AP satellite. Most of this stuff is commercial dreck--"Spice Girls look-alikes" or "Wayne Gretzky on high-definition TV"--but this might be a worthwhile source of clip art or material for parody. High-resolution downloads of the images are available free to the news media.
    Image Surfer Category List
    Images courtesy of Yahoo. Searchable and organized by subject. The quality varies.
    NAIL Homepage
    The National Archival Information Locator is a U.S. government website featuring more than 88,000 digitized photographs and other audio-visual materials which are available online and searchable by subject.
    US Government Documents: Audiovisual Information
    This is a page of links to government archives with downloadable graphic images.
    XPresso Bar
    An independent source of information and supplementary software for users of Quark XPress.

Health

    AMA Grassroots Political Action Home Page
    Visit this site to find out what legislation the American Medical Association is trying to promote.
    Biology and Community Health
    Articles and documents about how a variety of industries manipulate science and public opinion regarding public health issues including tobacco, breast implants, asbestos, etc.
    Biotech, Biotechnology and Pharmaceutical News,
    Cancer Times
    Political analysis of cancer prevention and treatment issues.
    drkoop.com
    A commercial website that rides on the reputation of former U.S. Surgeon General C. Everett Koop.
    Factors in the Emergence of Infectious Diseases
    This essay by Stephen S. Morse, Ph.D., explains how environmental and technological innovations have facilitated the emergence and spread of new infectious diseases such as HIV, mad cow disease, hantavirus and others.
    Holistic Healing
    This is the best site I've found for information on alternative healing. Mark Gold, the webmaster, is a frequent contributor to debates on internet newsgroups. Personally, my view is that advocates of holistic healing are better at critiquing the medical establishment than they are at offering healthy alternatives, but I still find their perspectives valuable and enlightening.
    Medscape
    Free online access to sources including MEDLINE, AIDSLINE, TOXLINE, Merriam Webster's medical dictionary, and medical news. This site might be a little technical for people who aren't medical professionals.
    MedWatch: The FDA Medical Products Reporting Program
    This site contains reports of adverse consumer reactions to drugs, nutritional supplements and medical devices.
    Medical websites
    A good basic list of links to online medical information, compiled by the Ontario Ministry of Health.
    Microwave News
    "The world's most exhaustive source on EMF health risks," according to the Washington Journalism Review.
    Navigating the Health Care System
    Bonner &Associates created this website for the Employer Quality Partnership (EQP), a coalition of employer organizations such as the Business Roundtable and National Association of manufacturers who want to foster "a market-based health care system"--as opposed to free, public health care.
    Longevity Research Center
    Commentary on food supplements by Dr. Ray Sahelian, an advocate of supplement use who has authored numerous books about melatonin, creatine and other popular nutritional supplements. I don't share his enthusiasm for supplementation, but he is a thoughtful observer who reads the research and doesn't simply cheerlead.
    Outbreak
    An information resource on emerging diseases, with sections on ebola, hantavirus, mad cow disease, dengue, malaria, smallpox and others. If you want to know what working scientists think of Richard Preston's The Hot Zone, check out this site.
    PubMed
    Searchable access to MEDLINE, offering 9 million citations and abstracts of medical articles, plus links to participating online journals and other related databases.
    The Ragged Edge
    News, commentary and political analysis for Americans with disabilities.
    U.S. National Library of Medicine (NLM)
    Sponsored by the National Institutes of Health, the NLM offers online access to numerous health-related resources on subjects such as AIDS, cancer, infectious diseases, biotechnology and toxic substances.
    United Silicone Survivors of the World
    A hefty compilation of information about health risks associated with silicone breast implants.
