Organizational Development

    Charity Search
    Earth Pledge Foundation
    Created by New York labor lawyer Theodore Kheel, Earth Pledge is an environmentally-oriented website. It also has a Division for Sustainable Media, which (I am told) raises money from the private sector to support development of websites for nonprofit organizations.
    Independent Sector
    Independent Sector calls itself a "membership organization that brings together foundations, nonprofit groups and corporate giving programs to support philanthropy, volunteering and citizen action." It's pretty milquetoast stuff that seems more geared to promoting the idea of volunteerism than offering anything specific to directly assist nonprofits. This website was irritatingly slow when I visited.
    John D. & Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation
    Dedicated to helping groups and individuals foster lasting improvement in the human condition, the Foundation seeks the development of healthy individuals and effective communities; peace within and among nations; responsible choices about human reproduction; and a global ecosystem capable of supporting healthy human societies. The Foundation pursues this mission by supporting research, policy development, dissemination, education and training, and practice. Its website includes some excellent links to other internet resources related to fundraising and other topics of interest to nonprofit organizations.

    National Committee for Responsive Philanthropy
    Nonprofit Resources Catalogue
    Links, organized by subject, to thousands of internet resources for nonprofit organizations, including information on fundraising, accounting, and other topics.

Public Relations Industry

    Advertising/Public Relations
    America Israel Public Affairs Committee
    Campaigns and Elections
    Caponigro Public Relations
    Media relations, crisis management, public affairs, investor relations, and other services. Their website includes a few articles expressing their thoughts on crisis management.
    Center for Media & Democracy
    I made this website! The Center for Media & Democracy is a nonprofit watchdog organization that monitors and exposes manipulative and misleading practices by the public relations industry. It publishes a quarterly newsletter, PR Watch, for which I serve as associate editor. Back issues are available online, along with other information about the center including reviews of our books.
    Communication Works
    One of the rare PR firms that actually specializes in working on progressive causes, campaigning for handgun control, and against the tobacco industry, nuclear power, and sweatshops.
    Compuserve Journalism Forum staff.html
    This page reveals that the moderator of Compuserve's journalism forum is Jim Cameron, president of the Cameron Communications PR firm.
    Copithorne & Bellows Public Relations
    Hi-tech PR firm whose clients include America Online, Iomega, IBM, Hewlett-Packard, DayStar Digital, NeXT. This site is noteworthy for its collection of humorous cartoons about the PR industry. My favorite is the one in which a superhero named "PR-Man" rewrites a news release from "Managers indicted on charges of sexual harassment" to "Executives take hands-on approach."
    The Crisis Coalition
    Cyberspace Public Relations
    The only think I know about these guys is what I see on their website, but I like the fact that they've added links promoting some worthwhile nonprofit organizations and causes. Their site also contains one employee's poignant remembrances of a relative who died of AIDS.
    DRGM Advertising & Public Relations
    A Nevada PR/advertising firm whose clients include a number of major casinos. Their website uses an annoying "silver mining" metaphor/motif that makes it confusing to search.
    Edelman Public Relations
    One of the world's largest PR firms, Edelman has managed to cultivate a superficially "liberal" image, but this hasn't stopped them from working for the anti-environmental Wise Use movement, among others. Their website includes some case studies of their "crisis management" work for clients such as Merck pharmaceuticals and Odwalla, the natural juice company that faced a crisis when some of its juice turned out to be contaminated with E. coli.
    Fleishman-Hillard
    One of the world's largest PR firms. Their website is searchable.
    Florida Public Relations Association Online
    This has FPRA's code of ethics online. I didn't find much else of interest, and you have to be a member in order to access many parts of this website.
    The Gallup Organization
    This site includes the results of some of Gallup's opinion polling.
    Global Warming Cost
    A website created by Bonner & Associates for the purpose of generating astroturf email opposing U.S. participation in a global warming treaty.
    Grassroots Strategies
    Grassroots Strategies is a "grassroots" lobbying and advocacy company affiliated with the PR firm of Hill and Knowlton. It has coordinated campaigns for national and state trade associations, labor unions, coalitions and some of the largest public relations firms in the world.
    The Institute for Crisis Management
    Issue Management Council
    iZoned.com
    This site helps people "put the Internet to use winning elections, influencing public policy, or building coalitions with others who care about the same issues they do. "
    Hill & Knowlton, International Public Relations & Public Affairs
    These are the fine folks who flacked for the government of Kuwait during the Persian Gulf War. Hill & Knowlton was also the architect of the tobacco industry's PR strategy, beginning in the 1950s, to evade responsibility for the deadly nature of its product.
    Intro to Social Influence
    IntelligentPR.com
    Investor Relations Asia
    Profiles of publicly listed south Asian companies, plus a free online Nexis search of articles pertaining to Asia.
    Investor Relations Information Network
    This site has online annual reports and other factbooks provided by numerous major corporations.
    Issue Dynamics Inc.
    A consumer affairs consulting firm specializing in grassroots organizing and Internet PR. Clients include Ameritech, Bell Atlantic, Novartis, the Alliance for Public Technology and the Democratic Party.
    Karwoski & Courage Public Relations
    Reputation management, crisis management. This website is done in a cutesey "medieval fable" style: "The king was concerned because the Queen was unpopular, and the town criers were saying the moat was unsafe..."
    Ketchum Public Relations
    One of the world's largest PR firms. Their site includes some analytical pieces, e.g., an article recommending PR strategies for "communicating with a hostile audience."
    Lexicon Communications Corp.
    This PR firm was founded by Steven Fink, who got his introduction to crisis management while working for the governor of Pennsylvania during the near-meltdown at Three Mile Island.
    Media Insider
    A website mostly for PR pros, with links to Profnet and PR Newswire, plus a newsletter of PR strategy tips.
    Media Relations Group
    For authors, entrepreneurs and businessmen, "leading-edge thinkers," companies and non-profit organizations. Clients have included the governments of China and Israel, IBM and Kellog.
    Medialink
    A major distributor of video news releases.
    The Merck Action Network
    This website is part of Merck's "grassroots" network to mobilize employees and other members of the "Merck family" to lobby politically on behalf of the company. It includes features that enable users to send e-mail to their elected officials (with copies forwarded to Merck, of course).
    The Museum of Public Relations
    Created by the Specter & Associates PR firm, this site offers a sanitized stroll through the industry's history.
    National Anxiety Center
    Created by Alan Caruba, a longtime flack for the pesticide industry, the National Anxiety Center ridicules environmentalism as "fear-mongering."
    National Capital Chapter of the Public Relations Society of America
    The PRSA is the largest PR professional organization in the U.S., and its largest single chapter is located in Washington, DC (surprise, surprise). This site has resources including links to other PRSA chapters and resource links for journalists.
    NewsCom
    Online source for free downloads of PR news release photographs.
    News USA
    News USA calls itself "a national news and feature syndicate located in Washington, DC that distributes news features, fillers and op-ed material to thousands of daily, weekly and community papers across the U.S."
    Offers "nationwide media coverage" to 10,000 newspapers, 6,000 radio stations, 1,000 TV stations. Their idea of "news" includes stories such as "You Can Set Your Table with Coca-Cola Collectibles," "Telecom Deregulation Will Bring Faster Internet Access," and "Enjoy a Caibbean Getaway." Many stories include downloadable photographs.
    Opensecrets.org
    Home page of the Center for Responsive Politics, which tracks the effect of money on the political process. Check here to find out who's giving to which candidate.
    Opinion: Spin is Not a Dirty Word
    An essay by a PR person for the University of Baltimore, arguing that journalistic "objectivity" is a myth. Everyone "spins," so instead of talking about "bias" vs. "objectivity," we should be talking about "honesty," "opennness" and "adherence to facts."
    On-Line Public Relations Home Page
    A great resource for information about the PR industry and related topics. This site includes links to online research sources for opinion polling data, plus powerful and specialized internet search engines, financial and medical information, etc.
    Porter/Novelli Public Relations
    A major PR firm that specializes in health and consumer PR. Porter/Novelli specializes in what it calls "cross-pollination," a polite euphemism for what others would call "conflict of interest." For example, by representing nonprofit cancer treatment organizations, it has been able to recruit them in defense of its other clients such as pesticide makers.
    PR Central/Reputation Management
    The online version of leading trade publications "Inside PR" and "Reputation Management." Of the two, "Reputation Management" offers the most interesting look into the PR worldview. Edited by Paul Holmes, its typical formula is to take a hot-button issue and argue, "even though the corporate position on this issue might seem unethical, the truth is more complex than the critics would have you believe." For example, "Child labor in sweatshops is an easy practice to condemn, but otherwise they might not have jobs at all." (I swear to God I'm not making this up. It was in their September/October 1996 issue.) For other interesting reading, check out their story on Chevron's practice drill to rehearse an oil spill.
    PR Newswire
    Online access to PR news releases.
    PR Web
    News releases organized by topic, plus a number of resources for doing public relations work.
    Propaganda
    Analysis, with current and historical examples, of rhetorical tactics often used by propagandists: guilt by association, glittering generalities, etc.
    The Psychology of Getting and Spending
    Public Relations Society of America (PRSA)
    The leading PR trade association. Their website has links to PRSA local chapters, many of which have their own newsletters.
    Public Communications Inc.
    A national PR firm. Their website lists a number of revealing case studies, such as their work to defeat health reform in Montana and to defuse criticism of infant formula makers' marketing practices in the Third World.
    Public Relations Agencies and Resources on the WWW
    Links to hundreds of PR industry websites throughout the world.
    Reputation Management Associates
    Their website includes a lengthy client list.
    Russell & Herder Advertising/Public Relations
    strategy-based firm serving clients in the U.S. and Canada.
    S&S Public Relations
    media relations for the medical, high tech, food and news media industries.
    Sanders Consulting Network
    clients are advertisers and public relations firms.
    Peter Sandman
    Sandman is a Rutgers professor and PR consultant to industry who expresses his central insight into risk communications with the formula: "Hazard + Outrage = Risk." He has a bunch of pseudoscholarly ideas for how companies can reduce outrage (without necessarily reducing hazards).
    SilverAnvil
    Each year the Public Relations Society of America issues "silver anvil" awards to PR firms for doing the best job of PR in a variety of areas ranging from new product launches to crisis management.Description of the campaigns that won the awards appear on their website.
    Stackig Advertising and Public Relations
    This company boasts that its crisis plan was the one that was used when the deadly Ebola virus was discovered in a laboratory in northern Virginia.
    US Newswire Current Headlines
    Online wire service distributing news releases, transcripts, and media advisories related to public policy.
    Western Fuels Association
    The Western Fuels Association, a coal industry propaganda group, campaigns to"debunk" scientific warnings about the dangers of global climate change.

Right Wing

    American Policy Center
    Run by long-time PR man Thomas DeWeese, this "think tank" goes heavy on the "tank" and light on the "think." Designed to appeal to the paranoid looney fringe of the far right, it runs vitriol-laden articles with titles like "Al Gore, You Can't Hide--We Charge You with Genocide," "Land Grabbing Secrets of the Nature Conservancy," "The Pagan Roots of Environmentalism" and "Civic Journalism: America's Dance with Nazi-style Propaganda."
    CalNews.com Home Page
    Capital Research Center
    Capital Research Center is a right-wing organization that "exposes liberal bias" in government and nonprofit organizations. It publishes Patterns in Corporate Philanthropy, which documents donations to environmental groups by major polluters such as Waste Management Inc. (The real purpose of such giving is undoubtedtly co-optation, not "liberal bias.")
    Christian Coalition
    Onward Christian soldiers -- marching for the God of mammon.
    The Heartland Institute
    A right-wing think-tank closely networked with others. Its publications include "Intellectual Ammunition," a bimonthly magazine "with original columns by experts from the Cato Institute, Heritage Foundation, and eight other leading think tanks."
    Peter W. Huber Home Page
    Institute for First Amendment Studies
    Advocating separation of Church and State, this website features regular, critical reporting on the activities of the religious right in publications including the Freedom Writer.
    The National Center for Public Policy Research
    Online access to right-wing "experts" on a variety of issues from the environment to health, education and welfare.
    National Committee for Responsive Philanthropy
    Reports on the activities of corporate-funded right-wing think tanks. Their September 1995 special report is particularly insightful.
    Political Research Associates Main Page
    PRA is a nonprofit research center that collects and analyzes information on anti-democratic, authoritarian and racist right-wing movements. Their website has searchable databases on hundreds of right-wing groups.
    Town Hall: Conservative News and Information
    Clearinghouse for right-wing political websites.
    Western Journalism Center
    WorldNetDaily
    Right-wing news and commentary. Up-to-date conspiracy theories, anti-environmentalism and tax revolt.
