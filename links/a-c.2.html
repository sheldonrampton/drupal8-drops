    WebActive: What's New in Activism Online
    WebActive is a project of Progressive Networks and calls itself an "up-to-date, electronic guide on the latest in activism and progressive politics on the internet." Progressive Networks is a corporate entity with clients such as ABC, Sony, AT&T, CBS, Time Life, Dow Jones, GTE, University of California, Microsoft, National Football League, U.S. Senate, Muzak, JC Penney, National Public Radio, Apple, Netscape and America Online. Investors include Rob Glaser, Mitchell Kapor (founder of Lotus Development Corporation), and Accel Partners, a venture capital firm. It has some interesting progressive content, but it appears to be designed with the intent of helping market the company's multimedia software.

Books

    Amazon.com! Earth's Biggest Bookstore Include in Search
    Amazon.com is a for-profit company that sells books online through its website. It has a wider selection than you'll find on the shelves of any conventional bookstore. Not only that, its website offers computer search facilities that let you search their database for books related to topics that interest you, and to read reviews by other readers. It also offers a discount on most books of around 20%.
    ABA Bookweb
    The American Booksellers Association home page has fresh book-related news, listings of other bookstores on the Web, and much more.
    BookZONE Include in Search
    BookZONE is like Amazon.com, but much less comprehensive and well-designed. The main thing worth visiting is its "Literary Leaps" page, which has thousands of links to book-related websites.
    Hungry Head Books--The Controversial Bookstore Include in Search

    Loompanics Include in Search
    This publisher calls itself the "lunatic fringe of the libertarian movement." Its online catalog includes how-to books on a variety of illegal, controversial or disreputable subjects ranging from bomb-making to tax evasion to "alternative health" to a guidebook titled "Top Cathouses of Nevada." Most of these books are unavailable in bookstores, and for good reason. These guys really know how to push the envelope of the First Amendment.
    Moneyblows.com Include in Search
