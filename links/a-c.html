My grandmother, Beatrice Marchant, was a Roosevelt-era Democrat whose activism sprang from the hard lessons of the Great Depression. She was the mother of 15 children (Mormons aren't big on birth control), and after they were grown up and her husband had passed away, she was elected to the Utah State Legislature. This poster is from her first election campaign in 1968.

Activism

    The Electronic Activist
    Who ya gonna call? The Electronic Activist provides an email address directory of congresspeople, state officials and news media for each U.S. state as well as for the federal government.

    Jay's Leftist and Progressive Internet Resources Directory
    One of the most comprehensive online directories of leftist resources on the internet.
    Progressive Resources Publications Home Page
    WebActive: What's New in Activism Online
    WebActive is a project of Progressive Networks and calls itself an "up-to-date, electronic guide on the latest in activism and progressive politics on the internet." Progressive Networks is a corporate entity with clients such as ABC, Sony, AT&T, CBS, Time Life, Dow Jones, GTE, University of California, Microsoft, National Football League, U.S. Senate, Muzak, JC Penney, National Public Radio, Apple, Netscape and America Online. Investors include Rob Glaser, Mitchell Kapor (founder of Lotus Development Corporation), and Accel Partners, a venture capital firm. It has some interesting progressive content, but it appears to be designed with the intent of helping market the company's multimedia software.

Books

    Amazon.com! Earth's Biggest Bookstore
    Amazon.com is a for-profit company that sells books online through its website. It has a wider selection than you'll find on the shelves of any conventional bookstore. Not only that, its website offers computer search facilities that let you search their database for books related to topics that interest you, and to read reviews by other readers. It also offers a discount on most books of around 20%.
    ABA Bookweb
    The American Booksellers Association home page has fresh book-related news, listings of other bookstores on the Web, and much more.
    Black Planet Online Bookstore
    Radical and anarchist books.
    Books for a Better World
    This is my very own "online bookstore," which lists and reviewes some of my favorite books, many of which focus on issues central to building a better world--peace, human rights, and the environment. It also includes a section featuring books that have been targeted for censorship or other types of suppression. Reading a banned book a day keeps the censors at bay!
    BookZONE
    BookZONE is like Amazon.com, but much less comprehensive and well-designed. The main thing worth visiting is its "Literary Leaps" page, which has thousands of links to book-related websites.
    Chapters Books
    Has lists of award-winning children's books.
    Eco Books
    Environmental book reviews, with a list of environmental websites thrown in for good measure.
    Frontlist Books: New Books for Scholarly Readers
    Emphasis on feminist, lesbian, cultural studies...heavily academic focus.
    Hungry Head Books--The Controversial Bookstore
    Brief reviews, with an emphasis on unpopular or controversial books.
    The Independent Reader
    Provides reviews and recommendations from several alternative bookstores.
    Left Bank Books
    One of the oldest leftist bookstores in the United States.
    Library of Congress
    Loompanics
    This publisher calls itself the "lunatic fringe of the libertarian movement." Its online catalog includes how-to books on a variety of illegal, controversial or disreputable subjects ranging from bomb-making to tax evasion to "alternative health" to a guidebook titled "Top Cathouses of Nevada." Most of these books are unavailable in bookstores, and for good reason. These guys really know how to push the envelope of the First Amendment.
    Moneyblows.com
    Intelligent, blunt reviews about business books. For an example, check out its review of The Art of Closing Any Deal, which tells the so-sad-it's funny story of an author who is begging people not to buy his book.
    New York Review of Books
    The On-Line Books Page
    Most notable for its page on "banned books online," which not only lists a number of banned book but has links showing where free copies of many banned books can be downloaded over the internet.
    Pentagon Book Store
    Find out what the guys in uniform are reading when they're not buying $300 screwdrivers.
    Words -- A Neighborhood Bookstore
    Books of interest to African-Americans.

Computers and Technology

    Aladdin Systems
    Makers of StuffIt and other file compression/encryption software. They offer free downloads of file decompression software.
    America Online
    I hate America Online. If I ever obtain a license to kill, Steve Chase will have a tag on his toe quicker than you can say, "disconnected."
    Apple Computer
    Beyond Computing
    A "solid source for industry news and reviews" which "goes right to the heart of the matter: how can all of this wonderful technology, software and hardware help your business succeed? . . . Many useful interactive services."
    The Center for Democracy and Technology
    This group deals with privacy and other issues associated with computers and technology.
    The CGI Resource Index: Programs and Scripts: Perl
    Perl is a popular computer programming language used to create interactive web pages. This site offers free downloads of already-writen Perl programs for many common tasks.
    Dr. HTML
    Free testing service for Web pages. Simply type in the URL you want checked, and it displays an analysis showing any dead links or other problems. This service does a very thorough job of checking, but only lets you check one page at a time, with a maximum of four pages per session. It has a for-pay service that will check an entire website for a reasonable fee. If you want to check a whole site, however, NetMechanic does a reasonable job for free.
    Download.com
    Searchable, well-organized site for downloading free computer software.
    Examples of Scripts Created with CGI.pm
    Another source of Perl programs.
    ExecPC
    The home page for my internet service provider. ExecPC also hosts "Filepile," a dowload source for free shareware.
    Experts Exchange
    Provides free online technical help and expert consulting on a variety of computer and internet topics.
    Fetch
    Go here to download "Fetch," an FTP program for the Macintosh. FTP stands for "file transfer protocol," and if you don't know what that means, you probably don't need it. It's used for downloading and uploading files over the internet. Your web browser can probably do the same thing, but Fetch is a little more efficient.
    Filemaker
    In my humble opinion, Filemaker is the best database software in existence. It's definitely the easiest to use for most purposes.
    How to Deal with Spammers
    Some helpful advice on how to deal with people who send you junk email.
    HyperSolutions
    Shareware, internet solutions and custom programming for the Macintosh.
    INFO-MAC HyperArchive
    Another site for downloading Macintosh shareware.
    MacPerl Homepage
    MERP: Macintosh E-Mail Resource Page
    A definitive resource for information on Macintosh email software.
    MkLinux: Linux for the Power Macintosh
    MMG: The Online Agency - Internet Advertising,
    Internet promotional services. This site has a helpful list of reviews of the "top 100 online search engines." Valuable for increasing your website traffic.
    NCSA Telnet
    A download site for Telnet. If you don't know what it is, you don't need it.
    NetMechanic: Online Link Testing, HTML Validation
    You can submit the URL for an entire website to NetMechanic, and it will run a free check of the entire website for bad links.
    RISKS-LIST: RISKS-FORUM Digest
    Discussions of all kinds of technological risks and hazards. This is a great place to visit if you want to compile a shopping list of dangers that the experts are ignoring.
    Shareware.com
    A well-organized site of software for all computer platforms.
    VirtualPROMOTE
    Lots of helpful resources for publicizing your own website, including a thoughtful "how-to" essay, a list of the most important places to list your site, and another list of 500 more idiosyncratic, targeted directories, each with a brief review explaining it.
    The WebMasters

    ZDNet Downloadables

Corporate Research

    Business Week Online
    Some of the information on this site is free to everyone. Some of it is free only to subscribers.
    CNN Financial Network
    Corporate Watch
    Contains helpful advice on how to research the activities and ownership of transnational corporations, including a well-organized guide with links to numerous online databases and other resources.
    Consumer Project Home Page
    The Consumer Project claims to be an organization which helps everday citizens defend themselves against corporate ripoffs and abuses. It focuses heavily on exposing unfair practices of the insurance industry. I don't know enough about this organization to offer an endorsement. Judging from the content of its website, I can't be sure, but I wouldn't be surprised to find out that it is sponsored by trial lawyers as part of their ongoing battle with the insurance industry. In any case, the website contains some interesting (and seemingly reliable) information.
    DataCenter Home Page
    Includes a monthly bibliography on the religious right and a guide to right-wing websites.
    Directory of Transnational Corporations
    Brief descriptions of numerous corporations; organized alphabetically.
    dowjones.com
    A free, searchable site from the publishers of The Wall Street Journal, featuring top business news, publications, web sites and industry topics.
    Edgar Online
    "The leading source of SEC filings and related business intelligence on the internet."
    Go Trade
    A website set up by Bonner & Associates for the Business Roundtrable, which advocates normalized trade relations with China.
    Money Online
    Information on investing and personal finance management.
    Public Register's Annual Report Service (PRARS)
    Corporate information, press releases and annual reports on over 3,000 public companies.
    Rockwell Ombudsman
    Relentless critiques of the Rockwell corporation, including environmental crimes such as unsafe disposal of nuclear waste as well as information about the company's political lobbying.
    Useful Links--National Investor Relations Institute (NIRI)
    This has a good, annotated list of online sources for information about tens of thousands of companies.
