Frands and Stena Petersen

great-great-grandparents of Sheldon Rampton

There are no photographs of Frands' parents, Hans and Maren Petersen, who perhaps should be considered the true beginnings of our Mormon family line. Their conversion to Mormonism occurred in 1861 when missionaries visited their home in ****, a Danish town near Copenhagen. In those days, Mormon converts were strongly encouraged to "gather to Zion" so they could build the Kingdom of God that Brigham Young was busily establishing in southwestern United States. Their departure in 1862 came as America was embroiled in its Civil War, and the apocalyptic prophecies of Mormon scripture could only have intensified their sense that they were embarking upon a journey into the very heart of horror. Joseph Smith, the founder of Mormonism, had prophesied that a day would come when "with the sword and with bloodshed the inhabitants of the earth shall mourn; and with famine, and plague, and earthquake, and thunder of heaven, and the fierce and vivid lightning also, shall the inhabitants of the earth be made to feel the wrath, and indignation, and chastening hand of the Almighty God, until the consumption decreed hath made a full end of all nations." In addition to "wars and perplexities of nations," the heavens themselves would be in turmoil: "For not many days hence and the earth shall tremble and reel to and fro like a drunken man; and the sun shall hide her face, and the stars shall refuse to give light; and the moon shall be washed in blood; and the stars shall become exceedingly angry, and shall cast themselves down as a fig that falleth off a fig-tree."
