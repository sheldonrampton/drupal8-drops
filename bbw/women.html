Women


The Bell Jar
By Sylvia Plath
This autobiograpical work eloquently tracks the author's descent into madness. Residents of Evansville, Illinois, challenged its use in school because it contains sexual material and advocates an "objectionable" philosophy.
Other Topics Related to This Book: Banned Books, Fiction & Poetry.

Between Us
A Legacy of Lesbian Love Letters
By Kay Turner, Editor
This is a delightful collection of love letters between women both historical and contemporary, ranging in spirit from poignant to passionate to humorous (the letter home from the Michigan Women's Music Festival is a riot!). Illustrated throughout with whimsical, full-color collages on a letter-writing theme.

Blue Jelly
Love Lost and the Lessons of Canning
By Debby Bull
Hearing the song "I'm so Miserable Without You, It's Almost Like I've Got You Back" and realizing it could be written about her, Debby Bull begins a serach for a cure for her broken heart. "I was driven to canning by the wreck of my heart" begins Blue Jelly. In the world of canning things turn out as they should, providing the courage to explore beyond the kitchen. Blue Jelly shows us that depression can lead to new adventures and that for anything to turn out, patience is required. There are fifteen canning recipes that you may want to copy for use. Ingredients for recovery are mingled among the canning ingredients.

Celebrating Girls
Empowering and Nurturing Our Daughters
By Virginia Beane Rutter
Isabelle Allende says of Rutter's book containing twelve nurturing rituals, "Women will find in these inspired pages guidance to help their daughters and granddaughters develop a sense of pride in being female."

Chore Wars
How Households Can Share the Work and Keep the Peace
By James Thornton
Are the battle lines forming in your house? Why are women always angry about their second shift at home? This fellow cared for his household without his wife and learned a lot about the complexities he'd never considered. This is a thought-provoking read and a wonderful book to discuss.

The Color Purple
By Alice Walker
Retained as an English course reading assignment in the Junction City, Oreg. high school (1995) after a challenge to Walker's Pulitzer Prize-winning novel caused months of controversy. Although an alternative reading assignment was available, the book was challenged due to "inappropriate language, graphic sexual scenes, and book's negative of black men." Considered inappropriate because of its "troubling ideas about race relations, man's relationship to God, African history and human sexuality." Challenged by Oakland, California, high school honors class, 1984; rejected for purchase by Hayward, California, school trustees.
Other Topics Related to This Book: Banned Books, Fiction & Poetry, Race and Ethnicity.

Crazy For Democracy
Women in Grassroots Movements
By Temma Kaplan
Temma Kaplan, a professor of history and women's studies at SUNY Stonybrook, has written a practical, inspiring book about women struggling for social change. She draws upon interviews and testimonies of six activist women deeply involved in creating better lives for themselves, strengthening their communities, and improving the environment. Her prose is down to earth, her analysis filled with hope.

Critical Race Feminism
A Reader (Critical America Series)
By Adrien Katherin Wing, Editor
Open to almost any page of CRITICAL RACE FEMINISM and you will discover a thoughtful, provocative essay on one of any number of issues facing black women in America. Lani Guinier, Anita Hill, and Patricia Williams are among the 40 contributors who comment on topics such as motherhood, welfare, domestic violence, gangs, and the interplay of race and gender. An excellent resource!

Daddy's Girl
Young Girls and Popular Culture
By Valerie Walkerdine
A look at how we see young girls, how they see themselves, and how popular culture mediates the view. The author looks at little girls on television and in the movies, in advertisements, and popular songs. She focuses on orphan figures such as Annie and Shirley Temple, plucky representatives of the working class who are the object of middle class charity, and how they affect real working class girls and their perceptions of themselves. This book is an original and thought-provoking study of a controversial topic.

Dancing in the Flames
By Marion Woodman; Elinor Dickson
A marvelous and magical exploration of the mysterious feminine that inexplicably takes the place of the "he" in God, becoming Goddess again and again in our imaginations and world views. A groundbreaking work in Jungian psychology and in the transformation of consciousness.

Defending Our Lives
Getting Away from Domestic Violence and Staying Safe
By Susan Murphy-Milano
Getting Away from Domestic Violence and Staying Safe. Murphy-Milano writes about some of the insights gleaned from her vast experience as the daughter of a mother murdered by an abusive father and the countless women she has gone on to counsel and protect as they have struggled against similar fates.

Derrida and Feminism
Recasting the Question of Woman
By Ellen E. Feder; Mary C. Rawlinson; Emily Zakin
This latest confrontation between feminism and deconstruction is productive, insightful, and compelling. Eight great thinkers together in one volume!

Facing the Mirror
Older Women and Beauty Shop Culture
By Frida Kerner Furman
Frida Kerner Furman, a professor of religion at De Paul University, explores the everyday world of older women who congregate weekly in a Chicago beauty shop. Written with a keen eye for detail and nuance, she compassionately charts how older women negotiate the terrain of aging, beauty, and nurturing friendships. Her skillful portrait reveals a setting filled with profound existential questions and critical concerns. A must read for those who work with and care for aging people as well as anyone interested in a fascinating ethnography.

Feminist Social Thought
A Reader
By Diana Tietjens Meyers
Diana Tietjens Meyers has gathered forty of the most compelling essays by the most influential feminist thinkers of our time into one indispensable volume. It includes excerpts from Sandra Bartky's "Femininity and Domination," Judith Butler's "Gender Trouble," and bell hooks' "Feminist Theory : From Margin to Center," as well as pieces by Carol Gilligan, Maria Lugones, Catharine MacKinnon, and Patricia Williams, to name just a few. Excellent as an introduction to the issues of feminism or as a reference for even the best-read feminist.

A Girl's Guide to Taking Over the World
Writings from the Girl Zine Revolution
By Tristan Taormino; Karen Green
This fascinating anthology of writing, art, and musings from the recent explosion of Girl 'zines--small circulation journals published by girls and young women--is an explosive demonstration of the power and energy of lives, the reality of which most of us have no conception. From first sexual encounters to paranoid fantasy, from boy problems to "Barbies We'd Like to See," this one's got it all.

Goddess of the Americas
Writings on the Virgin Guadalupe
By Ann Castillo, Editor
This anthology of writings on the patron saint of Mexico (and protectors of the downtrodden) explores the impact this potent deity has had on that country's history, politics, art, religion, and literature. Contributors include Sandra Cisneros, Clarissa Pinokla Estes, Gloria Anzaldua, Denise Chavez, and a number of other prominent Mexican-American writers.

I Am Beautiful
By Dana Carpenter, Editor; Woody Winfree, Editor
Hundreds of women responded to the query, "Tell us why you are beautiful." The result is a stunning collection of photos and quotations that reveal beauty at every age, size, and color.

In Search of Islamic Feminism
One Woman's Global Journey
By Elizabeth Warnock Fernea
To most Westerners, "Islamic Feminism" is a contradiction in terms. The author, who has spent 40 years traveling in and studying the Middle East, examines women's movements in various countries of the region. She speaks with women about how they see themselves in their societies, and whether they see their own struggles as part of the western-based women's movement. The answers she receives cause her to rethink her concept of feminism, what it means, and if it can be achieved.

Informed Consent
Nearly a million women in the United States have received silicone breast implants, and many of them are suing the manufacturers alleging health problems related to the implants. This book tells the story of John Swanson, an executive at the Dow Corning company, the world's largest implant maker. Originally a "company man" who refused to believe that the implants were harmful, he began having second thoughts when his wife suffered serious health problems after receiving implants herself. Swanson eventually felt compelled to resign from the company for reasons of conscience. Dow Corning's PR campaign has been successful in recent years in persuading many people that the implant issue is an unfounded "health scare." Before you make up your mind about the issue, you owe it to yourself to read this book.
Other Topics Related to This Book: Corporations, Health.

Inside Ms.
25 Years of the Magazine and the Feminist Movement
By Mary Thom
Mary Thom is trained as a journalist and historian. She was also involved with Ms. Magazine from its inception, first as a researcher and finally as executive editor. Ideally positioned to tell its story and analyze its impact on the feminist movement, Thom presents a lively and insightful history of this most influential of feminist periodicals.

An Intricate Weave
Women Write About Girls and Girlhood
By Marlene Miller, Editor
A rich and varied collection of stories, essays, memoirs and poems by women writing about the girls in their lives and in the world today, and also about their own girlhoods and the girl who remains within. This new anthology includes such acclaimed writers as Adrienne Rich, Lucille Clifton, Julia Alvarez, and Ursula K. LeGuin.

Kissing the Witch
By Emma Donoghue
A magical mythology that connects Cinderella, Snow White, Rapunzel and others like a family history that tells the stories of women we can all dream of being.

Men Doing Feminism
By Tom Digby, Editor
Feminist thought is at a pivotal time where it will be made or broken by its ability to survive the resurgence of conservatism and the backlash against feminism, feminists, and women in general. At this crucial moment MEN DOING FEMINISM steps forward to take on the daunting task of examining the inescapable interconnection between men, masculinity, and feminism. Each of these 18 essays is a daring and insightful exploration of the ways that masculinity can inform feminism or the ways feminism can transform masculinity. In this much-needed volume, Tom Digby has reopened an important conversation and made an excellent beginning to the project of showing feminism as the cooperative, progressive, inclusive movement it is.

My Life as a Boy
A Woman's Story
By Kim Chernin
A provocative, revealing memoir detailing the author's transformation from demure wife-and-mother into reckless adolescent boy. No, we're not talking about a physical transformation here; rather, a psychic one. Anyone who has had doubts about the applicability of standard gender stereotypes to our authentic selves should take a look at this.

Nothing Bad Happens to Good Girls
Fear of Crime in Women's Lives
By Esther Madriz
Subtitled: Fear of Crime in Women's Lives, Madriz study brings to the fore vital information that shows that crime rarely matches our beliefs about it. From the time we are young children, girls are taught to be fearful and vigilant. We are trained to be chaperones, that staying at home is safe, that we should fear strangers. Madriz argues that "fear of crime helps reinforce gender hierarchies in American society and organize consent about an appropriate code of behavior for women." This is a terrific book club read, leading readers into a complex discussion of our cultural roots in fear.

Our Bodies, Ourselves
By The Boston Women's Health Collective
This highly respected women's health manual has been removed from school libraries on numerous occasions. Reasons vary, but it has been called "pornographic" and "filthy," and was removed from the public library in Three Rivers, Michigan because "it promotes homosexuality and perversion."
Other Topics Related to This Book: Banned Books, Gay/Lesbian, Health, Sexuality.

The Penguin Book of Women's Humor
By Regina Barreca, Editor
Showcasing women's humor as a liberating social and political force, as well as an outlet for personal pain, Barreca has collected three centuries of women's humor encompassing a wide range of subjects. An uplifting antidote to the strains and stresses of everyday life as a woman, this book includes not only such comic talents as Dorothy Parker, Erma Bombeck, and Roseanne, but also writings by Emily Dickinson, Sylvia Plath and Toni Morrison. Regina Barreca, author of three previous books including Perfect Husbands ( and other fairy tales) has been deemed a "feminist humor maven" by Ms. and is one of the funniest and most entertaining speakers we've ever had at the bookstore.

Present Tense
Writing and Art By Young Women
By Calyx, Editor
An astounding chorus of new voices is raised in this groundbreaking anthology of art, poetry, and prose by young women in today's America. This book is as broad as its base, reflecting as many sexualities, ethnicities, and self-identifiers as exist within today's women's literary movement. A real prize, deserving of a broad audience.

The Price You Pay
The Hidden Cost of Women's Relationship to Money
By Margaret Randall
Margaret Randall discusses women's relationship to money and examines how cultural myths and stereotypes that paint women as financially incompetent facilitate domestic situations of control and abuse.

Public Privates
Performing Gynecology from Both Sides of the Speculum
By Terri Kapsalis
An insightful history and investigation of gynecology that exposes its racist, classist and sexist biases. Kapsalis, a health worker and performance artist, reframes the traditional gynecological exam as a performance with doctor and patient as co-directors, offering new possibilities for more egalitarian interactions. Thoughtful, confrontational, accessible and a must-read.

Real Majority, Media Minority
The Cost of Sidelining Women
By Laura Flanders
Public radio has led me to many interesting books. Last week I heard Laura Flanders of FAIR (Fairness and Accuracy in Reporting) talk about her book REAL MAJORITY, MEDIA MINORITY. It's a compilation of articles originally published in EXTRA!, FAIR's magazine, which deal with "the cost of sidelining women in reporting." Every person who believes that words and ideas matter, that there are justice-motivated intellectuals, that the truth is 'out there someplace' MUST read this book and rejoice in Laura Flanders, the work she (and others) are doing, the truth(s) she uncovers.

Sister to Sister
Women Write About the Unbreakable Bond
By Patricia Foster, Editor
No idealized, sugar-coated portraits of "sisterhood" here--this is instead an exceptional collection of honest, individual portraits of a wide diversity of relationships between sisters. Contributors include Lucy Grealy, bell hooks, Pam Houston, Kathleen Norris and Mona Simpson. Highly recommended!

Speaking Truth to Power
By Anita Hill
Six years after the nationally televised hearings in front of the Senate Judiciary Committee, Anita Hill finally tells her story.
Other Topics Related to This Book: Sexual Abuse.

The Story of Jane
The Legendary Underground Feminist Abortion
By Laura Kaplan
A fast-paced and fascinating look into the characters and locales of the early '70s, pre-Roe v. Wade underground abortion service called "Jane." A true page-turner, this book unfolds with stories of courage, hope, history and social change.

Straight, No Chaser
How I Became a Grown-Up Black Woman
By Jill Nelson
Nationally known columnist and author of VOLUNTEER SLAVERY, Nelson takes a candid look at what it means to be a black woman in America. She dispels the politicians' definitions of black female identity, and challenges readers of all genders and races to reconsider their views of black women.

To Be Real
Telling the Truth & Changing the Face of Feminism
By Rebecca Walker
Co-founder of Third Wave Direct Action Corporation and a contributing editor to Ms. Magazine, Rebecca Walker is also the editor of the anthology To Be Real, which explores young women's relationships with feminism and the complexities of female empowerment in the nineties.

Tracking the Serpent
Journeys to Four Continents
By Janine Pommy Vega
Janine Pommy Vega lives the kind of life which many of us desire. She sets out to connect to primal sources of the "Mother" with both abandon and purpose. It's no coincidence that sex is a necessary component of the greater connection. And her writing is absolutely fluid and lyrical--a real pleasure to read.

Urban Girls
Resisting Stereotypes, Creating Identities
By Bonnie Ross Leadbeater, Editor; Niobe Way, Editor
A long overdue collection of essays and studies about adolescent girls in urban environments. Urban Girls draws on new research from psychology, sociology, public health and education to fill in the knowledge gaps about young girls as they struggle to find their places in the world. The volume covers a wide range of topics, including sexuality and peer development and will be useful for scholars, educators, clinical workers, and anyone else interested in girls' development.

Wee Girls
Women Writing From an Irish Perspective
By Lizz Murphy, Editor
This collection provides a taste of contemporary writing by women of Irish heritage as well as a collective attempt to define what it means to be Irish. This moving and often funny collection includes fiction, poetry, and autobiography. Published by an Australian press, included are writers well-known to American audiences (such as Maeve Binchy, Mary Daly and Eavan Boland) as well as many writers who may be as-yet unfamiliar.

Whores & Other Feminists
By Jill Nagle, Editor
This lively and diverse anthology examines sex work and the sex industry through the eyes of self-identified feminist sex workers--strippers, dominatrices, prostitutes, porn writers, producers and performers--and their allies. Passionate, eloquent and articulate, this collection is guaranteed to expand your definition of feminism. Contributors include Nina Hartley, Liz Highleyman, Carol Queen, Annie Sprinkle, Candida Royalle and Marcy Sheiner.

The Woman's Wheel of Life
By Elizabeth Davis; Carol Leonard
Women who have turned to feminist spirituality have often struggled with the confining concept of the triple goddess. Carol Leonard and co-author Elizabeth Davis present thirteen empowering archetypes in their new book. From Daughter to Blood Sister, Mother to Amazon, Sorceress to Crone, this groundbreaking work reveals the grand pattern of women's lives, rich and complex, beautiful and mysterious. Both authors are midwives with over forty years combined experience in women's health, politics and spirituality. Carol Leonard is a foremother of the women's spirituality movement, co-founder of the Midwives Alliance of North America, and travels extensively presenting women's workshops on magic and healing.

Women Imagine Change
A Global Anthology of Women's Resistance from 600 B.C. to Present
By Eugenia C. DeLamotte, Editor; Natania Meeker, Editor; Jean F. O'Barr, Editor
Organized thematically and containing excerpts from diaries, letters, autobiographies, poems, essays, and memoirs, this book is a treasure-house of information on how women around the world over hundreds of years have resisted change and gained power.

Women in the Material World
By Faith D'Aluisio; Peter Menzel
Companion to Material World: A Global Family Portrait, this stunning book illuminates the differences and the similarities in women's lives all over the world, through photographs, first person interviews and statistics--all taken, conducted and compiled by women. A great choice for women's history month.

Women's Growth in Diversity
More Writings From the Stone Center
By Judith V. Jordan, Editor
A worthy successor to Women's Growth in Connection, this second anthology by writer and editor Judith Jordan continues to examine questions of women's development. This time, however, it is the differences among women, be they racial, cultural, socioeconomic, or involving sexual orientation, that are explicitly addressed.

Written By Herself
Women's Memoirs From Four Continents
By Jill Ker Conway, Editor
In this sequel to her best-selling collection, the author of Road From Coorain and True North gives us memoirs from three generations and four continents. She includes Vera Brittain, Isak Dinesen, a labor organizer who endured imprisonment and torture in South Africa, an Indian aristocrat who became an architect of her country's independent movement, and others who voice women's struggle for personal and political power, autonomy and inclusion over the course of the twentieth century. Harvard Book Store price $12.80.
