Aquamarine
By Carol Anshaw
Although originally published in 1992, Carol Anshaw's first novel was out of print for several years and has just been re-issued by Houghton Mifflin. We are very grateful for this, since it was always a favorite of ours to recommend and one of the most asked-for out-of-print titles. The novel explores the idea that life can look very different depending on the choices made at key turns, and that idea is developed by presenting three different versions of the main character's life. It succeeds wonderfully well, and Anshaw's wit and unique voice and style make reading this book a delightful as well as interesting experience. Because this book is provocative in many regards, it is a great choice for book groups.

Does Your Mama Know? An Anthology of Black Lesbian Coming Out Stories
By Lisa C. Moore
By turns funny, angry and joyous, this collection reflects the complexity of emotions that accompany a black lesbian's coming out. Forty-nine short stories, poems, interviews, and essays by contributors that include Jewelle Gomez, Makeda Silvera, Terri Jewell, and Becky Birtha.

The Escape Artist
A Novel
By Judith Katz
Ah, the magic of Judith Katz's writing! Her Lambda Literary Award-winning novel, RUNNING FIERCELY TOWARD A HIGH THIN SOUND, established her as a force to be reckoned with. THE ESCAPE ARTIST only deepens that conviction. Set in the brothels and gangster dens of turn-of-the-century Buenos Aires, this haunting and beautiful novel is a consummate exercise in literary sleight of hand. It's not for nothing that Katz has been described as a "Jewish Magical Lesbian Realist...."

Every Woman I've Ever Loved
Lesbian Writers on Their Mothers
By Catherine Reid, Editor; Holly Iglesias, Editor
An exciting new collection of tender, provocative, and painful reflections; there is great variety here in both perspective and style. The contributors range from emerging to established writers, and the poets, playwrights, fiction, and non-fiction writers included offer a full spectrum of insight into all mother-daughter relationships while revealing at the same time the intensity and uniqueness of relationships between lesbian daughters and their mothers.

Farm Boys
Lives of Gay Men from the Rural Midwest
By Will Fellows
"If there is a checklist to see if your kid is queer, I must have hit every one of them - all sorts of big warning signs. I was always interested in a lot of the traditional queen things - clothes, cooking, academics, music, theater. A farm boy listening to show tunes? My parents must have seen it coming." Joe Shulka, from an interview in Farm Boys.

Feminism Meets Queer Theory
By Elizabeth Weed, Editor; Naomi Schor, Editor
Lively in form as well as content, this collection includes interviews, discussions, critical exchanges and an author's response to a review of her book in addition to essays on the political, intellectual, and academic intersections of feminist and queer thinking. Judith Butler, Gayle Rubin, and Teresa De Lauretis are a few of the notable contributors to this provocative volume.

Gut Symmetries
By Jeanette Winterson
Jeanette Winterson is always wildly ambitious and innovative in her fiction, and this sixth novel is no exception. Here she draws on a range of ideas from physics and cosmology to medieval history in this story of three people involved in a complex love triangle. Old fans will be delighted and new converts made.

The History of Lesbian Hair and Other Tales of Bent Life in a Straight World
By Mary Dugger
Like lesbian Dorothy Parker, Mary Dugger is a humorist whose deliciously wicked vision is sure to amuse and provoke. In a sort of Lesbian Contract with America, she delivers an unrelenting assault on the straight world with an array of humorous essays, diagrams and short takes.

Hot, Throbbing Dykes to Watch Out For
By Alison Bechdel
The further tales of our favorite comic strip dykes. In this set, we learn more about the women's various hits and misses in the world of love, sex and intrigue.

The Last Closet
By Rita M. Kissen
The stories in this book tell about the daily lives of over a hundred lesbian, gay and bisexual women and men who are teachers, counselors, librarians and administrators across the country. The author, whose daughter is a lesbian teacher, describes the struggles of educators caught between their desire for authenticity and their need for safety, a need that forces many of them to hide a significant part of their identity. Kissen also suggests survival strategies for lesbian and gay educators and offers advice for heterosexuals who want to make their schools and communities inclusive and safe for all.

The Lesbian Health Book
Caring for Ourselves
By Jocelyn C. White; Marissa C. Martinez, Editor
The first comprehensive book of its kind, and published by a feminist press with a long history of ground breaking work, this collection of essays will save lives as well as improve them. With a keen awareness of the politics of health care, the contributors to this anthology (consumers as well as medical professionals, many of whom entered medicine because of bad experiences as consumers) discuss all the obstacles to getting quality care as well as practical ways to overcome many of them.

Lesbians Raising Sons
An Anthology
By Jess Wells
This thoughtful anthology includes essays by several dozen contributors, including such notables as Robin Morgan, Jennifer Levin, Audre Lorde, Lillian Faderman and Minnie Bruce Pratt. Dena Taylor, author of FEMINIST PARENTING, calls it "a poignant and down-to-earth book that makes you think and think again about what it means to raise sons in today's world. . .readers will be enlightened, challenged, sometimes saddened, often amused, and never bored."

The Necessary Hunger
By Nina Revoyr
The setting is contemporary Los Angeles with the two main characters being girl high school basketball stars heavily recruited by every major university basketball program in the country. One star's love for another is beyond the dimensions of the court but not a tender, sensitive courtly presence. This novel is the real thing in terms of basketball, atmosphere and young feminine yearnings for another. THE NECESSARY HUNGER breaks bounds in both the conventional literary and basketball senses.

Our Bodies, Ourselves
By The Boston Women's Health Collective
This highly respected women's health manual has been removed from school libraries on numerous occasions. Reasons vary, but it has been called "pornographic" and "filthy," and was removed from the public library in Three Rivers, Michigan because "it promotes homosexuality and perversion."
Other Topics Related to This Book: Banned Books, Health, Sexuality, Women.

Outlooks
Lesbian and Gay Sexualities and Visual Cultures
By Peter Horne, Editor; Reina Lewis, Editor
June is Gay Pride month, & set aside by the publishing industry to honor & acknowledge gay & lesbian contributions to our life & culture. This collection of essays explores the relationship of lesbian and gay sexualities to visual representation. It's a great place to start engaging with the questions of the relationship between sexuality and art, and to hear the compelling voices of artists and theorists who are actively engaged with those questions.

Particular Voices
Portraits of Gay and Lesbian Writers
By Robert Giard
This is a wonderful collection of photographs of 182 writers, each accompanied by an excerpt from his or her work. Those profiled represent a broad spectrum of writers from the past 75 years and a diverse range of literary genres as well. Robert Giard's pictures, taken in personal settings and using existing light, capture beautifully and evocatively the character of his subjects.

Sex Changes
The Politics of Transgenderism
By Pat Califia
SEX CHANGES is Pat Califia's engaging, thought-provoking, and meticulously researched analysis of the contemporary history of transsexuality. Califia chronicles the history of transgender autobiography from Christine Jorgenson to Mark Rees to Kate Bornstein, and examines how transgendered people's perceptions of themselves have changed, as well as how the attitudes of those outside the gender community have evolved. It also includes an impressive bibliography and list of resources--a compelling read!

Strong Women, Deep Closets
Lesbians and Homophobia in Sports
By Pat Griffin
Written by Pat Griffin, a lesbian athlete and coach, and with a foreword by the Executive Director of the Women's Sports Association, Donna Lopiano, this book is the first thorough exploration of homophobia and heterosexism in women's sports and their effects on women's lives. Given that the connection between lesbianism and athleticism is one of our oldest cultural stereotypes, the mere fact that it is only now being addressed suggests how loaded a subject it is. Griffin does a superb job of showing how homophobia interacts with sexism to limit women's full access to sports and therefore to all the benefits -- physical, psychological, and social -- that derive from participating in sports. This is a very important book for all those who live, work, and play in the world of sports, but also for those interested in girls' development and in the overarching issues of women's equality and social justice.

Sunnybrook
A True Story With Lies
By Persimmon Blackbridge
This is the hilarious and tragic tale of Diane, a woman who fakes her way into a job at "Sunnybrook Institution for the Mentally Handicapped," a choice that ultimately changes her life. A combination of visual art and fiction, this book is as interesting to look at as to read.

Too Queer
Essays From a Radical Life
By Victoria A. Brownworth
Long time activist and journalist Brownworth here articulates her vision of an American and gay radicalism. As she says, it is a book about the importance of radical ideas, the impact of living a radical life. The issues she raises are essential for all of us, gay & straight, political & apolitical. Challenge yourself, challenge your friends.

Virginia Woolf
Lesbian Readings
By Eileen Barrett; Patricia Cramer
This excellent collection of fifteen essays explores how Woolf's private and public experience and knowledge of same-sex love influenced her writing.

We Are Everywhere
By Merk Blasiun, Editor; Shane Pehlan, Editor
Presenting political, historical, legal, literary, and psychological documents which trace the evolution of the lesbian and gay movement, this comprehensive sourcebook includes documents as diverse as organization pamphlets, essays, polemics, speeches, newspaper and journal articles, and academic papers.
